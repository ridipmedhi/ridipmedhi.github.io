Research Overview

The primary focus of my mathematical research has been to provide a unified approach to the study of Iterated Function Systems (IFSs) defined by weaker contractive mappings. By relaxing classical, rigid contraction constraints, my work investigates the core structural, geometric, and statistical behaviors of non-linear chaotic systems, focusing heavily on invariant measures and fractal dimensions.

Key areas of contribution from my doctoral work include:

  • Generalized Contractions & HB-Theory: Established the Hutchinson-Barnsley theory, coding map continuities, and the explicit existence and uniqueness of invariant measures for broad families of non-standard mappings, including countable cyclic $\phi$-contractions, local radial contractions (LRCs), and $F$-contractions.
  • Elton's Ergodic Theorem & Banach Limits: Formulated the framework of ergodicity-inducing IFSs to derive fresh, sufficient conditions under which Elton's Ergodic Theorem holds. By integrating Lorentz's classic results on Banach limits, we corrected foundational inaccuracies in existing literature to construct a simpler, unified proof strategy.
  • Dimension Formulae for $\phi$-Similar Systems: Resolved an open restriction in the dimension theory of generalized self-similarity by proving the Hausdorff dimension formula ($dim_H(\Lambda) = s$) for $\phi$-similar sets under the Strong Separation Property (SSP) without assuming the exact Hausdorff measure is positive and finite. Additionally, derived exact values for the Hausdorff dimension of associated invariant measures.
  • Operator Dynamics: Evaluated the Ruelle operator theorem under systems with variable weight functions, confirming uniform convergence properties of normalized transfer operators.
